7.3.2 CXCL12’s actions

7.3.2.1 CXCL12 does not affect intrinsic membrane properties in pyramidal-like cells in the basolateral rat amygdala

As presented in Table 7.7 and Fig.7.9, treatment with CXCL12 had negligable effect on measured membrane parameters.

Figure 7.9: CXCL12 did not significantly change any of measured membrane properties of BLA principal cells.

7.3.2.2 No significant effects on excitatory synaptic transmission were observed following application of exogenous CXCL12

Data illustrated in Table 7.8 and Figure 7.10 indicate that exogenous CXCL12 did not affect excitatory synaptic transmission.

Figure 7.10: CXCL12 did not modulate excitatory synaptic transmission.

7.3.2.3 No robust effect of CXCL12 action on the GABAergic transmission in the BLA

CXCL12 does not induce any marked change in GABAergic transmission in the BLA (see Table 7.9 and Fig. 7.11).

Figure 7.11: CXCL12 does not modulate GABAergic transmission onto principal cells in the BLA

7.3.2.4 Besides enhancing excitability, CXCL12 does not seem to affect any other intrinsic membrane properties

Table 7.10 and Figures 7.12 and 7.13 report no prominent difference in membrane properties of CeL neurons.

Figure 7.12: No prominent changes in adaptation ratio, input resistance, membrane time constant, and cell capacitance are observed in CeL neurons after CXCL12 application.

Figure 7.13: No prominent changes in action potential width, AP amplitude as well as resting membrane potential are observed in CeL after CXCL12 application.

7.3.2.5 Effects of CXCL12 on excitatory synaptic transmission in the CeL

The detailed summary regarding CXCL12 actions on excitatory synaptic transmission in the CeL is presented in Table 7.11 and Fig. 7.14.

Figure 7.14: CXCL12 does not affect amplitude (a1-3) and kinetics (b1-c3) of sEPSCs in the CeL

7.3.2.6 Effects of CXCL12 on inhibitory synaptic transmission in the CeL

CXCL12 does not modulate kinetics of spontaneous and miniature inhibitory postsynaptic currents in the CeL, as shown in Table 7.12 and Fig. 7.15.

Figure 7.15: Kinetics of inhibitory postsynaptic currents are not modulated by CXCL12 nor AMD3100 in the CeL neurons
